1. Challenge Overview & Objective
I. Real World Context
Mathematical reasoning represents the absolute frontier of artificial intelligence capability. Providing a numerical answer to a problem is trivial when a model has memorized the training data; producing a mathematically rigorous, logically progressive derivation path, the "how" and "why" behind the answer requires genuine cognitive synthesis. Large models achieve this through massive scaling, but distilling this capability into constrained networks is the definitive target of modern AI research.

II. Objective
The goal is to generate flawless, multi-step mathematical reasoning traces that perfectly derive a given answer from a provided math problem. Participants must reconstruct the symbolic sequence (which heavily utilizes LaTeX syntax and algebraic reasoning) from base principles without resorting to rote guessing.

III. The Training Mandate: AIMO Distillation
PRIORITY FOCUS: This is a Model Training and Distillation Competition.
Evaluation will only proceed for strictly fine-tuned open-weight networks.

Model Constraint: You must fine-tune an open-weights LLM of 8 Billion parameters or fewer (e.g., Llama-3-8B, Mistral-7B).
Training Modality: We suggest Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) over curated reasoning traces or Reinforcement Learning (PPO/GRPO) to instill verifiable logical derivation.
2. Context Complexity
The challenge demands reasoning against several adversarial factors:

Symbolic Trace Degeneration: When reasoning algebraically, if step 3 of a 10-step derivation contains a mathematical hallucination (a dropped sign, a flawed partial fraction decomposition), the derivation degrades immediately. The model must learn self-correction or highly accurate forward passes.
OOD Difficulty Generalization: A model trained to solve basic algebraic identities will fail to generate proofs regarding combinatorics or number theory. The test evaluation performs stratified splitting across advanced olympiad-tier math.

II. The Adversarial "Math Entity F1" Check
MathEntityF1 (40%): Unlike standard text evaluators, this specific pipeline uses regular expressions and syntax parsing to extract numeric values, logical operators, and LaTeX macros (e.g., \frac, \int, \sum) from the ground-truth. To prevent "spam attacks" where models hallucinate massive dictionary payloads of numbers to artificially inflate their score, this metric strictly enforces Precision alongside Recall. It operates as the F1 harmonic mean of your perfectly recovered mathematical algorithmic tokens, heavily penalizing unneeded symbols masking as derivations.
