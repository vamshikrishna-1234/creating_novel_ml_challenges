## Overview

This dataset is fully synthetic, generated programmatically using NumPy and template-based text generation with a fixed random seed (42) for full reproducibility. It simulates a noisy LLM alignment evaluation pipeline where instruction-response pairs have been rated by multiple annotator personas with differing quality criteria.

The dataset contains 60,000 instruction-response pairs. Each instruction requests a specific format, topic, length, and constraint. Each response is generated with 5 hidden quality dimensions (factual consistency, constraint adherence, format compliance, verbosity match, and coherence) that are NOT directly observable as columns. Five hidden annotator personas independently rate each pair: three legitimate personas with different quality weighting functions, one adversarial persona that inverts quality ratings for approximately one-third of topic categories, and one noisy persona that blends two legitimate signals with additive random noise. Two personas share partial correlations in their weighting functions, making clean disentanglement difficult.

The ground-truth quality tier (0–4) is derived from the consensus of only the 3 legitimate personas, quantized at quintile boundaries. Numeric annotator scores and metadata are retained in the raw data for reproducibility but are NOT provided to solvers. The solver's goal is to predict the ground-truth quality tier for held-out test pairs using only the instruction and response text, recovering the true alignment signal from textual analysis alone.

## File Structure

- `data.csv` — The primary dataset file containing 60,000 instruction-response pairs with associated metadata and quality labels. Contains 8 columns: a unique identifier, the instruction text, the response text, a topic category index, the corrupted observed score, a response word count, a topic popularity metric, and the ground-truth quality tier.

## Notes

- Instructions are template-generated from combinations of 40 topics, 5 formats, 4 lengths, and 15 constraints.
- Responses are generated with 5 hidden quality dimensions: factual consistency, constraint adherence, format compliance, verbosity match, and coherence. These dimensions are NOT directly observable as columns.
- 5 hidden annotator personas rate each pair. Three legitimate personas weigh quality dimensions differently. One adversarial persona inverts quality ratings for approximately one-third of topic categories. One noisy persona blends two legitimate signals with additive random noise. Two personas share partial correlations (overlapping weight structures), making them harder to disentangle.
- The `observed_score` is the average of 3 randomly selected personas (selection is hidden). For some samples, this includes the adversarial or noisy persona, systematically corrupting the signal.
- Tier boundaries were set at quintiles of the consensus distribution, producing approximately balanced classes (~12,000 samples per tier).
- 15% of ground-truth tier labels have been shifted by ±1 (label noise), establishing an irreducible error floor.
- `response_word_count` and `topic_popularity` are red-herring features: correlated with `observed_score` in the dataset but not causally linked to the true quality tier.
