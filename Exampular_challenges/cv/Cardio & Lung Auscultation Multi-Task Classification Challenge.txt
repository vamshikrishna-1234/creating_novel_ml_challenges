1. Problem Overview
This competition presents a unified cardiopulmonary diagnosis task based on mixed acoustic recordings images.

Participants receive 224×224 log Mel spectrogram images. Each recording contains simultaneously captured heart and lung sounds, with and without signal separation.

The objective is to build model(s) that generate two independent clinical predictions from a single mixed spectrogram:

Heart condition classification (10 classes)
Lung condition classification (6 classes)
Both predictions must be inferred directly from the same mixed image. At test time, only mixed spectrograms are provided. No isolated heart or lung signals are available during evaluation.

Some heart–lung combinations in the training split are intentionally low-shot. A subset of category pairs contains only a single mixed training example. This introduces a controlled few-shot setting within the multi-task framework and increases the difficulty of robust generalization.

This creates an intentional training–inference asymmetry designed to promote representation disentanglement and multi-task learning approaches.

2. Dataset Summary
130 total mixed recording images (63 train, 67 test)
63 independent heart and lung recording images
10 heart condition classes
6 lung condition classes
The training split includes isolated heart-only and lung-only spectrograms. These auxiliary signals are provided strictly for training and are not part of the test set.

For every heart–lung combination that appears in the test set, at least one corresponding mixed recording is available in the training set as a reference example. This ensures that no test pair is entirely unseen, while still requiring generalization beyond a small number of observed examples.

4. Task Definition
For each mixed spectrogram image, predict:

heart_pred: one of 10 heart condition classes
lung_pred: one of 6 lung condition classes
Each prediction head outputs exactly one class.

All predictions must be derived directly from the mixed spectrogram.

5. Evaluation Metric: CLAMP Score
Performance is measured using the CLAMP Score (Cardio-Lung Aggregate Multi-Pathology):

CLAMP = 0.3 × MacroF1_heart
   + 0.3 × MacroF1_lung
   + 0.4 × Exact Match Ratio
Heart Macro-F1
Macro-averaged F1 across all 10 heart classes, with equal weight per class.
Lung Macro-F1
Macro-averaged F1 across all 6 lung classes, with equal weight per class.
Exact Match Ratio (EMR)
EMR measures the proportion of samples where both predictions are correct simultaneously:

EMR = (1 / N) × Σ 1(heart_pred_i = heart_label_i AND lung_pred_i = lung_label_i)
The EMR component introduces cross-task dependence into the final score.
