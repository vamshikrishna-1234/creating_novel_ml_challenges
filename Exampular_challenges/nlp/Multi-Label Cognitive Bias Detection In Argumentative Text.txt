Multi-Label Cognitive Bias Detection in Argumentative Text
Overview
You are given a dataset of argumentative text passages drawn from a range of policy and scientific domains. Each passage may contain zero, one, two, or three cognitive biases embedded within otherwise neutral, domain-specific argumentation.

Your task is to build a multi-label classifier that, for each passage in the test set, predicts which of 12 cognitive bias categories are present.

The 12 categories are: ad_hominem, appeal_to_authority, appeal_to_emotion, false_dichotomy, slippery_slope, strawman, hasty_generalization, circular_reasoning, red_herring, bandwagon, anchoring_bias, false_cause.

Biases are expressed through argumentative reasoning patterns, not surface keywords. A model that relies on vocabulary overlap with bias-label names will perform poorly. Domain-specific context surrounds each bias sentence intentionally — the model must understand argumentative function, not topic.
